IBM Founder Thomas J. Watson

Thomas John Watson Sr. was born in February 17,1874 in Campbell, New York as the fifth child and only on of Thomas and Jane Fulton White Watson.The son of lumber dealer, Watson studies at the Elmira School of Commerce and then worked as a salesman, first in a retail store then for a small cash register company. He then joined NRC in 1895 and eventually rose to the post of general sales manager of the company. In 1912 the president of NRC Mr.Patterson involved Watson in an illegal antitrust scheme that resulted in convictions for both men, later overturned. Watson left NRC in 1913. In 1914, Watson became president of the Computing-Tabulating-Recording Company which later changed its name to International Business Machines Coorporation in 1924. Watson was successful in making highly motivated, well-trained, and well-paid staff. He remained the chairman and CEO of IBM from 1914 to 1956. He was married to Jeanette M. Kittredge with whom he had 4 children including Thomas Watson Jr. and Arthur K. Watson. He dies on June 19, 1956, in Manhattan, NYC. When he died in 1956, he was one od the richest men of his time and was called the world's greatest salesman and a self-made industrialist.

Thomas had 4 children, two sons and two daughters who did later in their life were able to do significant works and put their name in the history as an individuals and not only as their dad's children. The work Sir. Watson's children did are listed down below:
